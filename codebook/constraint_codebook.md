## 1. `financial`

**Definition:** Constraints involving money, funding, capital access, or affordability. The venture faces binding financial limits.

**Representative phrases:**
didn't have money, no funding, ran out of cash, couldn't afford, limited budget, bootstrapped with no money, bank said no, couldn't raise capital, no revenue, cash flow problem, burn rate, runway, financially constrained, underfunded, couldn't pay, funding dried up, investors said no, no investment, out of money, financial pressure

---

## 2. `market`

**Definition:** Constraints from customer demand, competition, market size, or adoption barriers. External market conditions limit the venture.

**Representative phrases:**

## 3. `institutional`

**Definition:** Constraints from regulation, law, policy, compliance requirements, licensing, or government action.

**Representative phrases:**

## 4. `capability`

**Definition:** Constraints involving lack of internal knowledge, expertise, learning, or technical skill within the venture.

**Representative phrases:**

## 5. `talent`

**Definition:** Constraints about hiring, recruiting, retaining, or finding qualified individuals. The labor market or talent pool limits the venture.

**Representative phrases:**

## 6. `network`

**Definition:** Constraints about industry connections, partnerships, mentors, or ecosystem access. The founder lacks relational resources.

**Representative phrases:**
no connections, knew nobody, couldn't get a meeting, no access, didn't know anyone in the industry, no network, hard to get introductions, ecosystem not accessible, couldn't find a mentor, no referrals, doors closed, couldn't get to the right people, no relationships, isolated, outside the network

## 7. `self_imposed`

**Definition:** Voluntary constraints the founder adopts on the basis of values, ethics, mission, or principles. The constraint is chosen, not externally imposed.

**Representative phrases:**
we chose not to, based on our values, ethical line, wouldn't compromise, mission-driven decision, on principle, didn't want to, deliberately avoided, by choice, we decided not to, stayed true to, not willing to, refused to, wouldn't do it even if we could, values-based, we said no to

---

## Exclusion Rules

1. **Financial vs. talent:** "We couldn't afford to hire" → `financial` (binding constraint is capital, not labor market)
2. **Capability vs. talent:** "We didn't know how to do it" → `capability` (binding constraint is internal knowledge, not recruitment)
3. **Self-imposed vs. all others:** Any voluntary limit adopted for values or ethical reasons → `self_imposed`, regardless of surface similarity to other categories
